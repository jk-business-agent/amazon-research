Amazon Top Sellers & Rising Movers

July 2, 2026
Public web research · Cross-verified · 18 sources checked

Executive Summary

July 2, 2026 marks the first post-Prime Day report in this series, capturing the immediate aftermath of Amazon's four-day sale (June 23–26) that moved 307 million items and grew U.S. online retail spend 9.3% year-over-year to $26.4 billion per Retail Dive. The most striking finding: Premier Protein Shakes clinched the #1 overall Prime Day seller position — a direct signal that GLP-1 weight-management drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy) are meaningfully reshaping what Americans buy in bulk on Amazon, with high-protein, low-sugar ready-to-drink shakes replacing traditional grocery impulse buys. Household staples (Hefty trash bags, Cascade dishwasher pods) and grocery essentials (Liquid I.V., Temptations cat treats) dominated the early Prime Day charts, reflecting a consumer base that primarily used the sale for practical stocking-up — electronics captured only 14% of Prime Day shoppers versus 33% in 2025, a notable category retreat. A second distinct headline as of today: the Fourth of July 2026 is two days away, and it marks America's 250th birthday (1776–2026); America 250 anniversary apparel, patriotic garden flags, and cornhole sets for holiday gatherings are newly appearing on bestseller lists, representing a once-in-a-generation seasonal surge. Direct access to Amazon.com pages was blocked on all attempts (HTTP 403); all findings are sourced from retail trade press, consumer analytics firms (Numerator), and curated aggregators, as noted in the Methodology section below.

Methodology & Sources

All research conducted via WebSearch and WebFetch on July 2, 2026. Amazon.com pages were inaccessible on all attempts (HTTP 403 bot-detection blocks for amazon.com/gp/movers-and-shakers, amazon.com/Best-Sellers, and all category-level zgbs pages); this is noted as a degraded-mode run per the workflow protocol. Additionally, most major media outlet pages (NBC News, CNN Underscored, Retail Dive, Tinuiti, PureWow, Today.com, Numerator, BeautyMatter, FoodNavigator) also returned HTTP 403 on WebFetch; findings for those outlets rely on search-result snippets, which capture key statistics and product names but may omit full article context. Any specific number cited in a product card (dollar figure, ranking, count) was confirmed as appearing verbatim in a search result snippet attributed to the cited source — not reconstructed from memory or general context.

Verification rule: VERIFIED = corroborated by at least 2 independent sources. Amazon's own Best Sellers category page (confirmed active via search result links) counts as one source when applicable; a second press or analytics source is required. LOW-CONFIDENCE = only one source found for the specific product. Two products are marked LOW-CONFIDENCE today: FROGG TOGGS Chilly Pad and The Ordinary Niacinamide serum.

Key data anchor: Numerator's Prime Day 2026 consumer tracker is the primary source for Prime Day product rankings (Premier Protein #1, Liquid I.V. and Temptations Cat Treats top 3 Day 1 sellers, Hefty top Day 1 seller). This data is widely cited across retail trade press (storebrands.com, chainstoreage.com, foodnavigator-usa.com, retaildive.com) rather than accessible directly, but the cross-citation by multiple independent trade outlets provides strong confidence.
