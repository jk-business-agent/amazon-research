Methodology & Sources

Source data: Product list drawn from the sibling agent's July 6, 2026 report at ../Competitor Research Agent/docs/July6_2026/. 18 products identified; 5 Electronics category products excluded (per standing project rule — Electronics are never researched). 13 non-Electronics products were researched.

Verification standard: VERIFIED = 2 or more independent sources confirming American ownership AND American manufacturing (or assembly). The company's own website/marketing counts as exactly one source and is never sufficient alone. LOW-CONFIDENCE = company claim exists but only one source, or manufacturing is American-claimed but unconfirmed by any independent body, press, or public record.

Sort precedence: (1) NEW before RECURRING; (2) $25–$100 Amazon price band before outside-band products; (3) higher inherent repurchase interest before lower within each tier.

LMNT disclosure: LMNT is listed as VERIFIED for US corporate identity (confirmed by SEC EDGAR filings) but carries a material active disclosure: a class action lawsuit filed in Montana federal court in May 2025 (and parallel filings in NY and FL) alleges undisclosed maltodextrin in flavored products; unflavored "Raw" tested positive for 27 ppb lead in independent testing. This research does not assess legal merit. Sources: ConsumerLab.com and GetEvident.co. This disclosure must accompany any commercial recommendation involving LMNT.

ORCA Coolers: Previously cited as the leading American-made premium cooler brand. ORCA permanently ceased all operations in January 2025 per company announcement and Shop Eat Surf Outdoor trade reporting. Do not cite ORCA as a current or available alternative.

Jackery/portable power stations: No fully American-manufactured alternative paralleling the Jackery 1000 V2 exists. Over 80% of global lithium battery cell manufacturing is in China, South Korea, or Japan. Goal Zero is American-owned (NRG Energy subsidiary) but explicitly manufactured in China. Lion Energy assembles in Utah but uses imported cells. Grid Eraser assembles in Wisconsin with AGM batteries (Sun Xtender, California-made) but uses a Canadian-brand inverter and imported solar panels. All three are LOW-CONFIDENCE for "American-made" claims. Lion Energy's April 2026 equity stake in American Battery Factory (Tucson, AZ) may change this over time.

DREO evaporative coolers: Both American alternatives (Champion Cooler, Phoenix Manufacturing) are whole-home/whole-room installation units rather than portable desktop units like the DREO product being researched. Neither could be independently verified as "Made in USA" beyond the company's own claim and indirect trade-association listings. LOW-CONFIDENCE.

Nuun and Kate Farms (excluded): Nuun (Seattle) was acquired by Nestlé Health Science (Swiss multinational), transaction closed ~Q3 2021 — excluded as no longer American-owned. Kate Farms (formerly Santa Barbara, CA) was acquired by Danone (French multinational), transaction closed July 1, 2025 — excluded.
